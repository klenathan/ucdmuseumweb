Chinese Artifacts

Ancient China occupied a large and ever-changing geopolitical terrain, and the art it produced over three millennia reflects this diversity. Despite continual indigenous technical advancements, material and taste changes, and the impact of foreign ideas, there are certain characteristics inherent in Chinese art that enable it to be described in broad terms and recognized regardless of where, when, or for what purpose it was produced. These essential characteristics include an appreciation for nature, a belief in art's moral and educational capacity, an admiration for simplicity, an appreciation for accomplished brushwork, an interest in viewing the subject from a variety of angles, and a devotion to frequently used motifs and designs ranging from lotus leaves to dragons. Chinese art had a profound influence on its East Asian neighbors, and global acclaim for its achievements, particularly in ceramics, painting, and jade work, continues to this day.
