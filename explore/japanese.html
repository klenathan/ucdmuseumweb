Japanese Artifacts

Formulated in part on the basis of contemporary scientific judgments and in part on the basis of enthusiastic generalists' synthesis, these views on the qualities of Japanese culture and, more specifically, Japanese art, unsurprisingly reflected the prejudices and tastes of the period. The artistic inclination for refinement, for images that were discreetly imbued with metaphoric meaning, reflected the sublimely refined court customs that permitted only oblique references to emotion and favored suggestion over outright statement. Concurrent with the canonization of the Heian court style was the belief that the tea ceremony's aesthetic sensibilities were authentically Japanese. This 16th-century community ritual emphasizes the hyperconscious juxtaposition of found and carefully created things in order to induce subtle epiphanies of understanding. Additionally, it emphasized the centrality of indirection and understatement in the Japanese aesthetic.
